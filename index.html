Providing High Quality Education in Rural Areas While Creating Jobs for Rural Youth

Who We Are

Shiksha Saathi is a social enterprise dedicated to providing offline, high quality education in rural areas while creating jobs for rural youth, especially women. Through Vidya Centres, we deliver NEP aligned learning, coaching for students from Class 1-12, and skill-based programs that prepare them for higher education and careers. To ensure sustainability, we run educator training programs that enable rural youth to become teachers and trainers, generating local employment opportunities. Currently, we operate 29 centers, collaborate with 237 schools, and serve over 3,850 students. Recognized by UNDP India, our vision is to expand to 2000 centers in U.P. and launch an AI-powered App for learning and management.

Transforming the lives of Children

I am Archana Jaisal, Co-founder of Shiksha Saathi. I come from a small village in Ghazipur, where my father, once a rickshaw puller, worked hard to secure a railway job that gave me access to education in Banaras (BHU). His struggle inspired me to dedicate my life to bridging the rural-urban education gap so that children in villages don't miss opportunities simply because of where they are born. With a background in education and social innovation, I launched Shiksha Saathi to digitalize schools, coach students, and create jobs for rural youth, especially women. Recognized by UNDP India, I am committed to building an education ecosystem that makes rural children confident, skilled, and future ready.

Our Programs

Digital Classroom

We set up Digital Classrooms in remote rural government schools and Volunteers from across the globe teach the children in these schools for two hours a week in the regional language. Science, Math and English are taught along with Basics of Artificial Intelligence, Life skills and Leadership skills. Teaching aid is available to the Volunteer Teachers in the form of lesson plans, presentations and videos.

Teach Through Television

Curated video lessons in the regional languages are broadcast on local television channels in remote locations, based on a published schedule. This program takes advantage of the reach that local television has, hence delivering quality education in the last mile.

EmpowerED

Fostering Digital Literacy among rural students focused on Grades 6 to 9 in government schools.

BRAIN

eVidyaloka is collaborating with Microsoft to offer Artificial Intelligence courses to students in 300 schools for certain grades. The Project is named ‘Build Rural Artificial Intelligence Network (BRAIN).’

Vol of Fame

The passionate Volunteers at eVidyaloka come from all walks of life. They are Students, Homemakers, Working Professionals and Retired Individuals who can speak a regional Indian language and can teach online for two hours a week from the comfort of their homes and offices. Our volunteers are from across 50+ countries.
